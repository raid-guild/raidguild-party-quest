# Twist Guidelines

Use twists when:
- a natural `1` or `20` occurs
- tension is high
- the scene needs to branch
- the result would otherwise feel flat

## Twist Categories

Social:
- hidden witness
- accidental insult
- confession
- divided loyalties
- sudden bargain

Hazard:
- secondary collapse
- hidden safe route
- unstable artifact
- spreading danger
- short-lived opening

Combat:
- exposed weakness
- reinforcements
- collateral danger
- loss of footing
- chance to retreat

Mystery:
- clue reveal
- false lead
- memory echo
- hidden mechanism
- identity hint

Narrative:
- omen
- reality shift
- voice from elsewhere
- symbolic transformation
- time distortion

Twists should create decisions, not noise.
