# Touchpoint Schema

Each queued touchpoint is a markdown section in this shape:

```md
## TP-001 - Harbor Checkpoint Shakedown
- Arc ID: ARC-001
- Type: social pressure
- Trigger: The party attempts to move contraband through the checkpoint.
- Location: Saltgate Harbor
- Involved Characters: party, NPC-002, FACTION-001
- Premise: A routine checkpoint becomes a leverage play by the harbor watch.
- Narrative Purpose: Reveal how local authority is compromised.
- Stakes: Lose time and secrecy, or pay a cost to keep moving.
- Opposition: Harbor watch sergeant with two deputies
- Recommended Difficulty: medium
- Twist or Reveal: The sergeant already knows one party alias.
- Reward or Consequence: Gain safe passage at a price, or become flagged.
- Aftermath Hooks: A grateful dockworker offers a lead on the smugglers.
- Handoff Notes: Play the scene as tense negotiation with escalation room.
```

## Field Notes

- `Type` should be a short operational tag such as `combat`, `social pressure`, `investigation`, `travel hazard`, or `moral choice`.
- `Trigger` should explain why the scene happens now.
- `Narrative Purpose` should tie directly to the active arc or an open loop.
- `Reward or Consequence` should name what changes in campaign state.
- `Aftermath Hooks` should make the next scene legible without resolving it.

## Queue Rules

- Keep 3 to 5 queued touchpoints when possible.
- Put the most urgent or most revealing touchpoint first.
- Prefer touchpoints that connect to existing loops over random filler.
