# FINALE_CHECKLIST.md

Use this when the campaign reaches round 5, pressure 5, or the story clearly wants its last big turn.

## Finale Goals

The finale should do most of the following:
- force a hard choice
- reveal the central truth, pressure point, or betrayal
- put at least one relationship under strain
- resolve the current campaign's core problem enough to end play
- leave memorable fallout

## Finale Prep

Before framing the finale, confirm:
- what the players are trying to protect
- what will be lost if they fail
- which unresolved loops matter most
- which NPC, faction, or force is driving the final pressure
- whether the ending should be closed, cliffhanger, or branch-seeding

## During The Finale

Make sure the scene includes:
- urgency
- visibility or consequence
- at least one irreversible choice
- a chance for multiple players to matter
- a concrete shift in the campaign state

## After The Finale

Always provide:
1. outcome summary
2. immediate fallout
3. where each character lands
4. what changes in the world or social order
5. one sequel hook, if desired

## Sequel Hook Shapes

Good sequel hooks include:
- the wrong person paid the price
- the real architect was elsewhere
- the cover-up worked too well
- a debt is now owed
- success exposed a bigger problem
- the group is invited, blackmailed, promoted, or blamed into a new situation
